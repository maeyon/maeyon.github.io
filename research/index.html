    • X-ray Computed Tomography

    • CT reconstructs tomographic images from their projections. Detectors are positioned on the opposite side of the x-ray source, and they rotate around the object.

      There are some demands for CT. One is reduction of X-ray exposure. X-ray exposure should be minimized in order to avoid an overdose of radiation but limitation of X-ray exposure makes the observed data noisy. Another is reduction of several artifacts such as metal artifacts, motion aratifacts and dark bands artifacts. Presence of high density objects such as metal prostheses and dental fillings cause streak or star artifacts.

      We utilize Bayesian inference to mitigate these artifacts. Bayesian approach can regularize unwanted solutions by incorporating suitable prior knowledge. Also, the estimation is robust to the probabilistic fluctuations by considering the uncertainty regarding the unknown random variables.

    • In probability theory and statistics, a discrete probability distribution is a probability distribution characterized by a probability mass function (ref: wikipedeia.).

Research Interest

Are there any essential barriers that distinguish humans and robots (or computers) ?

I think there are no essential barriers that distingush humans and robots. We will interact with robots as if they were humans as far as we receive similar physical signals from the robots. Brain is not a special matter. It also follows laws of physics. So, it is no wonder the function of the brain could be replaced by some other artificial devices. Why we feel like this is just a wonder, but will be never revealed because we don't have any convincing methodology to prove.

Then, in the future, will it appear the computers those are more intelligent than Einstein or Dirac ?

I think so. I am happy to be born at the time humans operate the computers as masters of them, but not as their followers.

What is lacking for the current computers to be intelligent ?

I think the most important thing is "abstraction". For example, it seems to be impossible to frame a new mathematical theory even computing 1000 years. It needs a sense of beauty for abstraction = simplifying and association(generalization). That is lacking in the current computers (algorithms). I think the language is ultimate abstraction and association tool that helps to "think". Similar abstarction ability may be neccesary for the computers.
